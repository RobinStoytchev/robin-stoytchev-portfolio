About Me

Hey there! I’m Robin Stoytchev, a recent finance MSc. graduate based in Europe. I've created this page to share my insights into the financial industry, and other work that may benefit others interested in the industry. On this website you will find a mix of blog posts, useful finance douments, and more.

For unbiased media, we already have the internet. Thus, I will share my own genuine opinions on different subjects, hoping it can offer something to you as a reader.

Check out my projects to see my work in action. And please - don't be afraid to reach out! You can contact me on LinkedIn.

"This is the cover of my MSc in Economics degree certificate from Lund University. What inspires me the most is using data-driven analysis in combination with financial concepts, to reach actionable insights. These areas blend solid theory with real-world evidence, helping us understand how markets work and make smarter decisions. I have mainly focused on financial courses in my second cycle, while my first year consisted of macroeconomic literature."

Empirical finance uses data to test financial theories, revealing patterns in stock prices, risk management, and investment strategies. Foundations of Finance covers all the fundamental concepts of finance - variance, portfolio optimization, CAPM, risk-adjusted return, etc. I find this rewarding as these concepts are very much in use in practice, solving real problems for businesses and investors.

On a broader level, this degree has taught me the importance of critical thinking coupled with curiosity. Economic thinking and decision-making is highly correlated to the direction of society as a whole, as most people are presumably already aware of. How big do we want our government to be? Is spending on X really warranted given geopolitical state of Y? Or, how do I maximize my households long-term utility given our preferences?"
